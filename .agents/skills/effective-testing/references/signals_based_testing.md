# Signals-Based Testing (SBT)

Signals-Based Testing, as outlined in Chapter 14 of "Taking Testing Seriously," is a method of using AI-generated signals and system feedback to guide testing efforts. It's about leveraging high-volume data to identify areas of risk and failure.

## What are "Signals"?
Signals are pieces of information generated by the system, tools, or users that provide clues about the system's status:
- **Crashes and Hangs**: Obvious failure signals.
- **Assertion Failures**: In-code checks that were triggered.
- **Performance Markers**: Slowdowns, memory spikes, or resource leaks.
- **Problematic Patterns in Events**: Anomalies in logs or telemetry.
- **Feature Coverage**: Metrics on which parts of the code were executed during a session.

## How SBT Fits with RST
- **Not Automated Testing**: SBT is a way to *amplify* the tester's ability to observe.
- **Heuristic-Driven**: The human tester decides which signals are worth investigating and which are "noise."
- **Deep Investigation**: A signal is a *clue* that leads to an exploratory session.
- **AI-Generated Workload**: AI can be used to generate diverse, high-volume workloads to "shake" the system and produce signals.

## Getting Started
1. **Instrument the Product**: Ensure the system generates meaningful logs, telemetry, and assertions.
2. **Collect Signals**: Use tools to gather and aggregate these signals in real-time or post-session.
3. **Analyze the "Noise"**: Spend time filtering out known behaviors to focus on the truly interesting anomalies.
4. **Follow the Signal**: When a signal is found, start a SBTM session to investigate it deeply. Ask "Why did this happen?" and "What else could be affected?"

## Does it relate to the HTSM?
Yes. Signals are a form of **Information** (Project Environment) and help you understand the **Structure** and **Function** (Product Elements) more deeply.
